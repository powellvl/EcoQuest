Rules

In EcoQuest, 4 players compete as entrepreneurs to create the most sustainable and innovative digital project. By collecting Bonus cards, resolving Problems, and winning Quiz, players earn points. The player with the most points at the end of 5 rounds wins the game!

Game Setup

Game Board: A square track with 24 spaces card draw spaces, quiz spaces,discard spaces (discard one bonus card in your hand) Pass your turn (you can't play for the next turn). You can use a digital dice created just for the game, you can use it by clicking on the let's play button

Shuffle the three card decks and place them on their position to the board. Each player chooses a token and places it on the starting space. Each player starts with 3 Green Bonus card. The goal is to create the best digital project by the end of 5 rounds by collecting the most points through Green Solutions, resolving penalties, and winning Quiz.

Each player takes turns following these steps: Roll the Dice: Move your token forward the number of spaces rolled. If you land on a Card Draw Space, pick a card from the corresponding deck:

The cards

Bonus Card: Add points to your project or use them to resolve Penalties. These cards stay in your hand and can only be shown at the end or to counter a penalty card. When used to erased a panalty, this card is lost.

Penalty Card: Subtract points, penalty Cards represent problems or challenges in your project, such as increased costs or environmental issues. These cards reduce your points unless you take action to resolve them. The only way to resolve a Penalty Card is to use a contrary Bonus Card that directly addresses the issue. The points on the card are subtracted from your score.

Quiz Card : Launch a Quiz questions that you need to answer, if the answer is true, you can steal a card to another player, the amount of cards depend on the difficulty of the question, if the answer is false, you draw a penalty card. The amount of cards you can steal depend on the quiz difficulties.

The difficulty of the quiz is marked by the number of stars

1 star stand for easy, you can steal one card from another person if your answer is true, if not, pick 1 penalty card.

2 stars stands for medium you can steal two card from another person if your answer is true, if not, pick 2 penalties card.

3 stars stands for difficult you can steal three card from another person if your answer is true, if not, pick 3 penalties card.
